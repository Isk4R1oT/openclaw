# Conference Prep

Prepare for ML conferences as attendee, presenter, or author.

## As Presenter

### Abstract Writing
- Problem + approach + key result in 200 words
- First sentence: hook (why this matters)
- Last sentence: main contribution claim
- No jargon that isn't in the title

### Poster Design
- Title should be readable from 3 meters
- Flow: left-to-right, top-to-bottom
- Max 3 key figures
- Results table with baselines highlighted
- QR code to paper/repo

### Talk Structure (20 min)
- 0-3 min: Problem motivation (why should audience care?)
- 3-8 min: Background (minimal, just enough)
- 8-15 min: Method (core contribution, with diagrams)
- 15-18 min: Results (key experiments, ablations)
- 18-20 min: Conclusions + future work

### Q&A Handling
- Repeat the question for the audience
- "Great question" is okay once, not every time
- "I don't know" is better than making things up
- "We haven't explored that yet" is honest and fine
- Prepare answers for: "why not try X?", "how does this compare to Y?"

## As Attendee

### Planning
- Review accepted paper list before the conference
- Identify: 5 must-attend talks, 10 interesting posters
- Schedule workshops and tutorials on day 1
- Use conference-tracker skill to cross-reference with curriculum

### During Conference
- Take notes in structured format (store in study-session logs)
- Ask questions at posters (great networking)
- Summarize key talks for study log

### After Conference
- Write up 3-5 key takeaways
- Add papers to reading queue
- Follow up with interesting people
